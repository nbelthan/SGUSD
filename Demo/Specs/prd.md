Product Requirements Document (PRD): SGUSD "SageBridge" Demo1. Strategic Overview: The "Why"This section outlines the macroeconomic themes and business drivers justifying the SGUSD (Sagecoin) initiative.A. Industry Trajectory & ThemesThe money movement industry is shifting from Batch-Based Banking (ACH/Swift) to Programmable Streaming Liquidity.Verticalization of Finance: Software platforms are replacing traditional bank portals as the primary financial interface for SMBs.Tokenization of Real-World Assets (RWA): The use of regulated, yield-bearing stablecoins to bypass traditional correspondent banking, eliminating FX friction and settlement delays.B. Customer Problems AddressedThe "Idle Capital Gap": SMBs lose purchasing power every day their cash sits in non-interest-bearing operating accounts.Cross-Border Friction: When SMBs pay international contractors, they lose 3–5% on FX markups and wait 3–5 days for settlement.The Value Proposition: SGUSD turns idle operating capital into a productive asset (yielding interest) while enabling instant, zero-fee global payouts.C. Benefits to Sage & Ecosystem Connection ("Think Big")Sage currently sits on a disjointed ecosystem: 100M Consumers and 10M SMBs. Furthermore, 80% of payouts currently leave the platform, and 10% bleed out via FX.The Closed-Loop Vision: SGUSD acts as the Unified Social Ledger. By giving users a financial incentive to keep funds on-platform (yield + instant settlement), Sage captures the float and transaction volume that currently leaks to external banks.Bridging the Gap: We change the interaction model. When a consumer pays an SMB invoice, or an SMB pays a contractor, it routes through the Sage internal network via SGUSD rather than external card networks (avoiding the 3% interchange fee).D. Product RecommendationStrategy: Do not build a new standalone app. SGUSD should be built as an Embedded Treasury Module integrated directly into the existing Sage SMB and Consumer software surfaces.Immediate Action: Launch this functional MVP demo to prove the zero-fee remittance corridor and the "Yield-on-Capital" mechanics to stakeholders.2. The MVP Demo Narrative: "Show, Don't Tell"The demo will follow Acme Inc. (a US-based SMB) and their interactions within the Sage ecosystem.The Demo Flow:Consumer Payment: A consumer pays a $10,000 invoice. Acme Inc. receives this instantly in SGUSD.Instant Yield: Acme Inc. watches their $10,000 balance "tick" upward in real-time, proving their capital is actively working for them while sitting on the platform.Global Payout: Acme Inc. needs to pay a $5,000 invoice to Global Logistics (a contractor in another country).Zero-Fee Settlement: Acme selects the contractor from the Sage directory and hits "Pay." The funds settle in <2 seconds with zero gas fees (sponsored by Sage).Receiver Experience: The demo splits to show the contractor receiving the 5,000 SGUSD, which immediately begins accruing yield in their localized wallet.3. Functional UI/UX RequirementsTo visually communicate the strategic value, the MVP must include the following interface components:Requirement 1: The "Ticking" Treasury DashboardHigh-Precision Balance: Display the SMB's SGUSD balance with at least 6 decimal places (e.g., $10,000.045219).Live Accrual: The decimals must flutter/tick upward smoothly in real-time to visualize the programmable yield.Savings Realized Counter: A dynamic metric showing "Fees Saved vs. Traditional Banking" (calculated against a standard 3% FX + $45 wire baseline).Requirement 2: The "Sage Network" VisualizationVisual Routing Map: When a payment is initiated, display a simple node-based map showing funds moving directly from "Acme Inc." to "Global Logistics."Confirmation Toast: "Transaction settled 100% within the Sage Trust Network."BaseScan Verification: A persistent link attached to every action allowing the user to view the actual on-chain transaction on the Base network.Requirement 3: Traditional vs. Sage Toggle ("Ghost Mode")Mechanism: A toggle switch on the payout screen.Traditional Mode: Simulates standard UI—shows a 3-day ETA, a $45 wire fee, and a 3% FX markup.Sage Mode: Instantly updates the UI to show a <2 second ETA, $0 fees, and 1:1 settlement.Requirement 4: Frictionless Onboarding (Account Abstraction)Invisible Web3: No seed phrases or MetaMask popups. Wallets are generated instantly via email using embedded wallet infrastructure.Sponsored Gas: The user must never see a gas fee. All transactions are routed through a Paymaster.4.
